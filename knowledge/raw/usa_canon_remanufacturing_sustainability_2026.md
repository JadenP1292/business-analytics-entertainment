# Canon U.S.A. Launches Remanufactured Multifunction Devices for Sustainability

**Source:** Canon U.S.A. Newsroom
**URL:** https://www.usa.canon.com/newsroom/2026/20260407-product
**Date:** April 7, 2026

Canon U.S.A. announced the official launch of its multifunction device remanufacturing business in the United States, with operations underway at Canon Virginia Inc. The launch introduces the imageRUNNER ADVANCE DX C5800 RM Series.

## Products Launched

- **imageRUNNER ADVANCE DX C5860i RM** — up to 60 pages per minute (ppm), color A3 MFP
- **imageRUNNER ADVANCE DX C5840i RM** — up to 40 ppm, color A3 MFP
- Estimated parts reuse rate of over 90% per unit
- Remanufactured to like-new performance and quality (comprehensive diagnostic testing, cleaning, replacement of key components)

## Sustainability Initiative

This initiative is part of Canon's Kyosei philosophy of social and environmental responsibility:
- Reduces electronic waste (e-waste)
- Uses standardized global remanufacturing processes
- Canon Virginia Inc. serves as U.S. manufacturing/remanufacturing base

## Canon Quote

"This launch marks a significant milestone in Canon's commitment to environmental efforts. By bringing our proven global remanufacturing processes to the U.S., we are providing our customers with high-quality, reliable imaging solutions that can contribute to their own sustainability efforts." — Kiyoshi Oka, EVP and GM, Marketing Strategy Unit, Canon U.S.A., Inc.

## Competitive Significance

Canon's imageRUNNER ADVANCE DX line directly competes with Xerox's enterprise MFP lineup (XRX) and HP's imagePRESS/LaserJet Enterprise products. The remanufacturing initiative differentiates Canon on sustainability — an increasingly important enterprise procurement criterion — while maintaining competitive pricing vs. new-device alternatives.
