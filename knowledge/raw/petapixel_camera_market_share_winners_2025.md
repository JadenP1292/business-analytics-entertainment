# Sony, Kodak, and DJI Were the Big Camera Market Share Winners of 2025

**Source:** PetaPixel (Jeremy Gray)
**URL:** https://petapixel.com/2026/01/19/sony-kodak-and-dji-were-the-big-camera-market-share-winners-of-2025/
**Date:** January 19, 2026

Based on the annual BCN Awards (BCN+R), which tracks best-selling photography brands across the majority of Japanese electronics and camera stores.

## Mirrorless Camera Market Share (Japan, 2025 BCN Awards)

| Rank | Brand | 2025 Share | 2024 Share | Change |
|------|-------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Sony | 29.9% | 35.8% | -5.9 pp |
| 2 | Canon | 27.4% | 26.0% | +1.4 pp |
| 3 | Nikon | 15.1% | 14.5% | +0.6 pp |

Sony remains #1 in mirrorless but lost nearly 6 percentage points of market share. Canon and Nikon each gained modestly. Canon at 27.4% vs. Sony's 29.9% is a narrow gap — Canon is close to overtaking Sony as Japan's top mirrorless brand.

## DSLR Camera Market Share (Japan, 2025 BCN Awards)

| Rank | Brand | 2025 Share | 2024 Share | Change |
|------|-------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Canon | 44.3% | 69.4% | -25.1 pp |
| 2 | Nikon | 38.0% | 20.9% | +17.1 pp |
| 3 | Ricoh Imaging | 17.7% | 9.7% | +8.0 pp |

Canon still leads DSLRs but by a significantly smaller margin. Canon now down to just two DSLR models (5D IV + Rebel T7/Kiss X90 in Japan). DSLR category is a fast-shrinking segment.

## Compact Camera Market Share (Japan, 2025 BCN Awards)

1. Kodak — 24% (repeat winner; PixPro FZ55 was Map Camera's best-selling digital camera of 2025)
2. Fujifilm — 13.9%
3. Kenko Tokina — 13.3%

Kodak's dominance in compacts explains its strong stock price performance (KODK +81% over 90-day period in this analysis) despite being largely a brand licensing play. Budget PixPro cameras with decade-old tech are outselling premium Japanese offerings in Japan.

## Video/Action Camera (Japan, 2025)

- **Video cameras:** DJI 64.7% share — up to 48.1% market share gain vs. prior year
- **Action cameras:** DJI 40.1%, Insta360 37.9%, GoPro 18.9% (down from 34.3%)

China-based manufacturers now dominate action camera market the U.S. once led.

## Key Takeaway for Canon Competitive Analysis
Canon holds strong #2 position in mirrorless (27.4%, close to Sony's 29.9%) and still leads DSLRs. Kodak's compact camera dominance and KODK stock surge are driven by low-cost PixPro brand licensing, not fundamental imaging business strength — this is consistent with the 5x volatility vs. peers signal in the stock data. Sony's declining mirrorless share despite a maturing market suggests an opportunity for Canon to gain leadership.
