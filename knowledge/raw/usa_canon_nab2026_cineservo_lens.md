# Canon U.S.A. Empowers Modern Cinematography with CINE-SERVO 40-1200mm Lens at NAB 2026

**Source:** Canon U.S.A. Newsroom
**URL:** https://www.usa.canon.com/newsroom/2026/20260415-lens
**Date:** April 15, 2026

Canon U.S.A., Inc. announced a significant expansion of its professional imaging ecosystem with the introduction of the CINE-SERVO 40-1200mm T5.0-10.8 lens and a comprehensive firmware update for the Cinema EOS lineup (C400, C80, C70, C50, and R5C). Canon's parent company generates approximately $29.5 billion in global revenue and has ranked in the top-10 for U.S. patents granted for 42 consecutive years.

## CINE-SERVO 40-1200mm T5.0-10.8 Key Features

- **Broad Reach:** Covers 40–1200mm (world's longest focal length for servo zoom lenses compatible with large-format sensor cameras), with built-in 1.5x extender reaching 1800mm
- **Portable Design:** 14.6 lbs., 16 inches in length (PL mount version); same ring positions as predecessor for rig compatibility
- **Native Super 35mm sensor coverage** with integrated 1.5x extender for full-frame
- **RF Mount Communication:** Enables Dual Pixel CMOS AF II, Focus Guide, Auto Exposure Ramping Compensation with EOS C400
- **Virtual Production Ready:** Supports Zeiss eXtended Data and Cooke /i Technology (PL mount)
- **Estimated retail price:** $79,999 — shipping September 2026

## Cinema EOS Firmware Highlights

- **Auto Exposure Ramping Compensation** (EOS C400): overcomes iris ramping during zooms
- **Remote Operation:** USB control protocols for C400, C80, C50 (REC, Iris, Shutter, ISO, Focus)
- **Stream Reliability:** SRT auto-reconnection if signal momentarily lost
- **Enhanced UI:** Level display changes color when perfectly leveled (gimbal/tripod workflows)

## Strategic Significance

This announcement reinforces Canon's aggressive expansion into wildlife, sports, live events, cinema, houses of worship markets. Canon showcased at NAB 2026 (booth #C3825), Las Vegas, April 19–22 — the premier exhibition for broadcasting and video production equipment.
